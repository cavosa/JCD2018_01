The Solution Proposal

The threefold design solution to combat inundation

  • The rising sea levels are fast becoming an overwhelming force for Sydney’s coastal. Our design confronts this issue through a practical yet still distinctive approach in order to better the future for life on Sydney’s magnificent coastline. Our proposal is threefold: the initial solution of a dam wall provides the foundation to integrate the ancillary road and terraced farm idea to nurture the reenvisioning of a coastal lifestyle.
  • The design along Collaroy Beach was formed to explore the theme of a ‘universal’ space for Collaroy’s multiple uses. The structure has been proposed to replicate a vital organism expressing its internal forces through the various elements working together with the nature of materials used to generate authentic forms mirroring Collaroy’s residential, commercial and transport requirements.

Mapping the Sea Wall

  • Situated along one of Sydney’s finest beaches, will be a 4.5km long structure that confronts three significant concerns associated with coastal Sydney: rising sea levels; bottleneck peak hour traffic and lacklustre design aesthetics.
  • One of our ancillary issues was the transport bottleneck along Spit Road that has, for decades, plagued northern beaches residents with countless, wasted precious time. By raising the transport network above ground level to avoid the peak hour traffic, the bridge element of our design creates a sensation more suited to the coastal lifestyle.
